# Hi, I'm John.

I started my career studying computer science and building software systems. I’ve always loved understanding how complex systems work—whether that system is a database architecture, a distributed API, or something more abstract.

Recently, I moved to Seattle. And... I saw some messed up stuff.

In less than six months, I called 911 7 times and performed CPR twice. I saw one woman get hit by a car, and saw a man die in an alley. Gunfire, stabbings, too many overdoses to count. It was a heck of a crash course.

And it made me realize that I need to be doing something different with my life.

I’m currently transitioning from the world of software development into medicine. I'm now a certified EMT, and this year I'll start applying for PA school. Along the way, I’m documenting the process—what it feels like to change careers, what skills transfer from technology into healthcare, and what I’m learning about medicine from the ground up.

This blog is where I think in public. Some posts will be technical, some reflective, and some simply observations from the field.
